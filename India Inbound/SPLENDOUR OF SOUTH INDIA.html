Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival Chennai

Welcome to Chennai, capital of Tamil Nadu. Upon arrival at Chennai, we meet you on arrival and assist with your transfer to the hotel where you spend the night.

Day 2: Chennai - Mahabalipuram (60 Km/ 1 Hr.)

In the morning, we tour Chennai visiting the Fort St. George, the National Art Gallery and the Government museum. In the afternoon, we drive to the Mahabalipuram. This tiny village by the sea is famous for its stone carvings. Upon arrival check-in at the Resort. Spend the evening at your leisure. (B, D)

Day 3: Sightseeing of Kanchipuram & Mahabalipuram

Breakfast at the Hotel. After Breakfast set out on excursion to Kanchipuram - just 71 kms from Chennai, Kanchipuram is the golden city of a thousand temples and one of the seven sacred cities of India. Its many gopurams (Monumental Tower- usually ornate) can be seen while approaching the city from a long way off. Kanchipuram was successively the capital of the Pallavas, the Cholas and the Rayas of Vijaynagar. During the 6th and 7th centuries, some of the best temples here, were built in by the Pallavas. The city was also a great seat of learning and sri Sankaracharya, Appan, Siruthonder and the great Buddhist Bhikku, Bodhidharma lived and worked here. Today, it is known not only for its temples but also for its handwoven silk fabrics. Return to the Hotel. Afternoon excursion to Mahabalipuram. An ancient coastal port, south of Chennai, world famous for its shore temple, Mahabalipuram was the second capital and sea port of the Pallava Kings of Kanchipuram, the first Tamil dynasty after the fall of the Gupta Empire. The whole of Mahabalipuram is a fabulous open-air museum of sculptures in living rock. Monolithic shrines, scooped out caverns magnificently sculpted within and the world’s biggest Bas relief in rock, are truly wondrous examples of the sculptor’s art. The famous temples of the Rathas , the Penance of Bhagirath, the Shore Temple and the eight Mandapams (Shallow, rock cut halls) are also located at the beach. Return to the Hotel for overnight stay. (B, D)

Day 4: Mahabalipuram - Bengaluru (350Km/ 7hrs)

After breakfast, checkout from the hotel and drive to Bengaluru. The City of Gardens founded in 1537 by Kempe Gowda, a chieftain who constructed a mud fort at this site. Hyder Ali rebuilt it in stone in the 18th century. On arrival, we check in at the hotel for an overnight stay. (B, D)

Day 5: Bengaluru - Mysore (200Km/ 4Hrs)

In the morning we explore Bengaluru, the Garden City of South India. En-route to Mysore we visit Channapatna, a small hamlet famous for its lacquerware. Check in at the hotel in Mysore. (B, D)

Day 6: Mysore sightseeing

Tour Mysore, the City of Palaces. See the palaces, gardens, avenues and sacred temples. The city retains its old world charm and was the host during the Golden age of the regionally characteristic Carnatic music and dance. This afternoon visit the island fortress town of Srirangapattinum on the banks of the Kaveri River. In the evening, we visit the 1062m high Chamundi Hill Temple. This 2000-year-old temple is dominated by a seven storey, 40m tower. Take in the breathtaking view from the hilltop. Overnight at hotel. (B, D)

Day 7: Mysore - Mudumalai (110Km/ 2.5Hrs)

After breakfast, drive to Mudumalai. Check in at the hotel. We spend the afternoon on a bush trek exploring the jungles of the national park, one of south india’s largest wildlife reserves. Depending on the season you may visit elephants feeding. Overnight at hotel in Mudumalai. (B, D)

Day 8: Mudumalai - Coonoor (70Km/ 2.5Hrs)

After a morning safari in the park we drive to the hill station of Ooty at an elevation of 2286m in the Nilgiri Hills. In the afternoon we board the steam train to enjoy a trip to Coonoor. We have some spectacular views of the precipitous eastern slopes of the rain forested Nilgiris. Overnight at hotel in Coonoor. (B, D)

Day 9: Coonoor - Cochin (290Km/ 7Hrs)

We drive this morning to Cochin, one of the finest natural harbors in India. It has been a port of call for foreign traders since ancient times. Ancient mariners from Arabia, Holland, Britain, China and Portugal have all left their mark on this beautiful island city of Cochin. Check in and overnight stay at hotel in Cochin. (B, D)

Day 10: Cochin City Sightseeing

After breakfast we explore “God’s Own Country,” the commercial hub of Kerala with a tour of Cochin visiting the Dutch Palace, the Jewish Synagogue, the St. Francis Church and the Chinese fishing nets. The afternoon is at your leisure and in the evening, attend a performance of Kathakali, the famous dance drama. Overnight in Cochin. (B, D)

Day 11: Cochin - Alleppey (60Km/ 1.5Hrs)

After breakfast we travel to Alleppey for an overnight cruise through paddy fields, streams and canals. Evening at your leisure on board. Overnight stay on House Boat. (B, L, D)

Day 12: Alleppey - Marari (15Km/ 0.5Hr)

Breakfast on board before we disembark at Alleppey and drive to Marari. This excursion will appeal to the discerning traveller who is seeking something extra special in beautiful, natural surroundings. Marari originates from the name Marrarikulam, a picturesque fishing village on the Malabar coast line. On arrival check in at the hotel. Spend the rest of your day at leisure. Overnight stay at hotel in Marari. (B, D)

Day 13: Marari - Day at Leisure

Free day at your leisure to discover the lush coconut groves, the pristine white sand beaches and endless vistas of the Malabar shoreline. Overnight at hotel in Marari. (B, D)

Day 14: Marari - Cochin Airport (80Km/ 2Hrs)

You have the day at your leisure until the departure time. We transfer you to Cochin Airport to connect to your onward flight, with wonderful memories to be cherished for a lifetime. Your wonderful vacation comes to an end! (B)
