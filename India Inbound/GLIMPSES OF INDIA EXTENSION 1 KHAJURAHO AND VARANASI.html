Itinerary

Day 1: Delhi - Jhansi – Khajuraho

After early Tea / Coffee Proceed to the railway station with a packed breakfast Depart Delhi by the Shatabdi Express train at 08.00 Hrs. Arrive Jhansi at 10.45 Hrs. After arrival visit Jhansi and Orchha. Jhansi is a historic city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. On the banks of the Pahuj River. Orchha is a town in Madhya Pradesh state. The town was established by the Rajput King Rudra Pratap Singh in the 16th century AD. After the visit Proceed to Khajuraho (180 KM/5Hrs). Arrive Khajuraho and Check in, overnight at the Hotel. (B, D)

Day 2: In Khajuraho

After breakfast visit The Western Group of Temples, the Eastern Group of Temples and the Southern Group of Temples. These temples are dedicated to several Hindu Gods and Goddesses along with deities of the Jain religion. Among the temples that are standing till now, 6 are dedicated to Lord Shiva, 8 to Lord Vishnu, 1 each to Lord Ganesha and the Sun God, while 3 are dedicated to Jain Tirthankaras. The largest of the temples is the KandariyaMahadeo Temple that is dedicated to the glory of Lord Shiva. It makes Khajuraho one of the four holy sites dedicated to the glory of Lord Shiva, the other three being Gaya, Kashi and Kedarnath. Return to the hotel. Afternoon at leisure. Overnight at the hotel. (B, D)

Day 3: Khajuraho – Varanasi

Breakfast at the hotel. After breakfast Transfer to the Airport and Depart to Varanasi. Arrive Varanasi and Check in at the hotel. In the afternoon, visit the famous city of Sarnath, north east of Varanasi – it is as holy to Buddhists as Varanasi is to Hindus. Having achieved enlightenment at Bodhgaya, Buddha came to Sarnath to preach his message of the way to Nirvana. Later, Ashoka, the great Buddhist Emperor erected magnificent Stupas and monasteries at Sarnath. What is visible today are the ruins of a once flourishing Buddhist monastery and a fine museum which houses an excellent collection of Buddhist art and sculptures. Return to the Hotel and Overnight (B, D)

Day 4: Varanasi – Delhi

Early morning enjoy Tea and coffee at the hotel. Proceed for a boat ride on the river Ganges. The best time to visit the Ghats is at dawn when pilgrims take their morning dip. The most famous Ghat is the Dasaswamedh Ghat from where we begin this tour. Enjoy the boat ride while looking out for people on the Ghats – the women bathing discreetly in their sarees, the young men displaying prowess in yogic postures & the Brahmin priests offering prayers for one and all. Amidst all this you may also come across the burning Ghats which are cremation sites for the dead. Return to the hotel for Breakfast. After breakfast Proceed to the Airport Varanasi – Delhi flight. Arrive Delhi, return to the hotel for an overnight stay. (B, D)

Day 5: Depart Delhi

Breakfast at the Hotel. After Breakfast check out from the Hotel. Your wonderful vacation comes to an end! Proceed to Delhi International Airport to connect to your onward flight, with wonderful memories to be cherished for a lifetime. (B)
