Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival Delhi

Welcome to Delhi, the National Capital of India. Our representative will meet you outside the Customer Hall and transfer you to the hotel. Check in at the Hotel. Rest of the at leisure. Overnight at the Hotel (D)

Day 2: Delhi - Sightseeing

Breakfast at the Hotel. After Breakfast we set out for a full day city tour of fascinating Old and New Delhi. Morning sightseeing tour of Old Delhi begins with a visit to Jama Mosque, the largest mosque in India, built in 1650 AD. This is followed by a drive past the Red Fort (Closed on Mondays) (1639-48), built by Emperor Shah Jahan, creator of The Taj Mahal. Later proceed to Raj Ghat - the site of the memorial to Mahatma Gandhi. Afternoon sightseeing tour of New Delhi begins with a drive past Safdarjang’s Tomb (1753 AD) while we are on the way to the Qutab Minar (1193 A.D.). This striking medieval tower of victory, built by the slave dynasty, is 73 m high. Within the Qutab complex, amidst the ruins of Quwat-ul-Islam mosque, stands the Iron pillar which has stood the vagaries of weather and has not rusted for over 1500 years. Continue your tour to visit the Humayun’s Tomb. Later drive through the Embassy area, Diplomatic enclave, stop for a quick photo shoot at India Gate and Rashtrapati Bhawan (Presidential Estate formerly Viceroy’s House) and Connaught Place, New Delhi’s main shopping area. Overnight at Hotel. (B, D)

Day 3: Delhi - Mandawa (250 KMs/ 6 Hrs) / Half day Sightseeing

After an early breakfast proceed to Mandawa. After around 6 hours journey by road you will reach Mandawa. Upon arrival check in at the Hotel. This Afternoon visit the fort of Mandawa founded in the 18th century. Thakur Nawal Singh, son of Shardul Singh, founded the fort in Vikramsamvat 1812 (1755 AD). The fort dominates the town with a painted arched gateway adorned with Lord Krishna and his cows. Mandawa is home to fascinating mansions (havelis) that have lavishly painted walls. After the visit of the fort proceed to various mansions like Hanuman Prasad Goenka Haveli, Goenka Double Haveli, Murmuria Haveli, Jhunjhunwala Haveli, Mohan Lal Saraf Haveli, Gulab Rai Ladia Haveli, The BinsidharNewatia Haveli, LakshminarayanLadia Haveli and Chokhani Double Haveli. Also visit a few neighboring villages.Return to the hotel for an overnight stay. (B, D)

Day 4: Mandawa - Pushkar (225 kms/ 5hrs)

Journey through the scenic states of Haryana and Rajasthan to Pushkar. Upon arrival, check in at hotel. In the evening visit the Famous Brahma Temple, the Ghat and the bazaars. You can perform pooja at the temple. Overnight at Pushkar. (B, D)

Day 5: Pushkar - Udaipur (298 kms/ 5hrs)

This morning, we travel to Udaipur. After check in at hotel, the afternoon is yours to explore this fascinating city on your own. We meet later tonight for dinner. (B, D)

Day 6: Udaipur Sightseeing

Surrounded by hills and mountains, Udaipur is also known as the City of Palaces, a fairyland and a Vision in a Dream. Its beautiful palaces are mirrored in the calm waters of Lake Pichola.Enjoy the tour of Udaipur, visiting the City Palace, the Jagdish Temple and the Sahelio ki Barri (Ladies Garden). In the afternoon, enjoy a serene boat ride on Lake Pichola. (B, D)

Day 7: Udaipur - Jodhpur (260kms/ 4hrs)

After breakfast we visit the world famous Jain Temples at Ranakpur. Next, we rejoin our coach and make our way to Jodhpur, a city built at the foot of the hills amidst the Thar Desert. This ancient city is protected by nearly 10kms of fortified high walls with eight gates leading out of it. We check in at the hotel upon arrival. (B, D)

Day 8: Jodhpur - Jaisalmer (326kms/ 5-6hrs)

After breakfast our sightseeing tour of Jodhpur takes us to the Mehrangarh Fort, the Jaswant Thadas, the Moti Mahal and the Phool Mahal. Later we drive down to beautiful Jaisalmer, where we will stay at next two nights at hotel. (B, D)

Day 9: Jaisalmer - Sightseeing

Jaisalmer is called the Golden City because of the color imported to its stone ramparts by the setting sun. Our tour today takes us to the Fort, the 12-15th century Jain temples, the Patwon Ki Haveli, Salim Singh and Nathmalji Ki Haveli. In the evening, you enjoy the thrilling experience of a camel ride over the dunes at sunset. Enjoy dinner at the camp before returning to the hotel. (B, D)

Day 10: Jaisalmer - Bikaner (333kms/ 5hrs)

Today we make our way to Bikaner where we visit the 16th century Fort housing old palaces, temples and a mosque. Later we check into hotel. (B, D)

Day 11: Bikaner - Jaipur (354kms/ 5hrs)

After breakfast we travel to Jaipur, the vibrant capital of Rajasthan, popularly known as the Pink City. After check in, it’s time to relax for a while. Later we visit the Bazaar in the old part of Jaipur before we return to the hotel. (B, D)

Day 12: Jaipur Sightseeing

After an early breakfast, we climb up to Amber Fort, on elephant back or by jeep. Then it’s on to one of the renowned carpet or gem factories of Jaipur. In the afternoon, we visit the City Palace and Jantar Mantar bobservatory. Later in the evening we savor Rajasthani cuisine in a traditional village atmosphere at Chowkhi dhani. (B, D)

Day 13: Jaipur - Agra (233kms/ 4hrs)

In the morning on our way to Agra, we visit the enchanting deserted city of Fatehpur Sikri. This ancient capital of Mogul Empire was built by Emperor Akbar but was later abandoned due to the lack of a dependable water supply. In the afternoon, we visit the Tomb of Itmad ud Daullah and check into the hotel. (B, D)

Day 14: Agra - Delhi (205kms / 4hrs)

After our sunrise visit to the Taj Mahal you will understand why it is one of the wonders of the world and considered the ‘Love of a man epitomized in stone.’ After this unforgettable visit, we return to the hotel for breakfast before visiting the Agra Fort. Later we drive down to Delhi where we get together with our travelling companions for a dinner. Overnight stay at hotel in Delhi. (B, D)

Day 15: Depart Delhi

Breakfast at the Hotel. Your wonderful vacation comes to an end! Proceed to Delhi International Airport to connect to your onward flight, with wonderful memories to be cherished for a lifetime. (B)
